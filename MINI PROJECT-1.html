Ugadi is believed to be the day when Lord Brahma, the Creator in Hinduism, began the creation of the universe. Hence, it is seen as the start of a new cosmic cycle.

Historical Significance:

Ugadi has been celebrated since ancient times, following the lunar calendar (Panchanga). It falls on the first day of Chaitra month (Marchâ€“April), symbolizing the arrival of spring and harvest season.
